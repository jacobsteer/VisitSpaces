What is NASA?
The term NASA is an acronym and stands for The National Aeronautics and Space Administration. NASA is a global leader in space exploration, they are the ones researching, exploring, and investigating space for us to learn more about our galaxy!

They are based in the United States of America and have 20 centres across the nation. Many explorations are set out by NASA including ones on the moon and Mars! NASA helps us understand the other planets better but also ours too!

History of NASA
NASA started back on October 1st, 1958 and had 4 main sectors: aeronautics research, science, space technology and human exploration and operations.

One of the first major projects NASA worked on was Project Mercury to see if it is possible for humans to survive in space. The next was Project Gemini, which perfected the system which helped humans go to the moon in the 1960s.

NASA was created in reaction to the launching of Sputnik by the Soviets in 1957. Since its origin in 1958, NASA has been working hard in space education and exploration, click below to see more!

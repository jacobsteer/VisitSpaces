Our Solar System
Welcome to the one and only solar system!
This is the place where all the planets and celestial objects reside.
Scroll down and visit each part and learn something new!
The Sun
The largest and centre of our solar system. The Sun allows for earth to have life! Using its strong gravitational pull the sun keeps all the planets together in one system!

Classification: Star
Star Type: Yellow Dwarf
Temperature: 5,500°C - 15 Million °C
Atmosphere: Hydrogen and helium
Mercury
This is the smallest planet in our entire solar system! Mercury is the planet which orbits closest to the sun and is a part of the inner terrestrial planets. Even though Mercury is small it spins the fastest at 47 KM per second!

Classification: Planet
Planet Type: Terrestrial
Moons: 0
Temperature: -180°C to 430°C
Atmosphere: Has a thin exosphere made of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium
Venus
Also known as Earth’s twin due to its size and density, Venus is the second planet from the sun. One single day on Venus lasts 243 Earth days yet a year is only 225 Earth days! Venus is also a part of the inner terrestrial planets and spins backwards, which is unique compared to the other planets!

Classification: Planet
Planet Type: Terrestrial
Moons: 0
Temperature: 480°C
Atmosphere: Mainly carbon dioxide
The Earth
This is the planet we call home and is the only planet that can support life! It is third from the sun and is apart of the inner terrestrial planets. It is also the only planet on the solar system to currently have liquid water. In comparison to other planets, we should be happy we live on Earth!

Classification: Planet
Planet Type: Terrestrial
Moons: 1
Temperature: -25°C to 45°C
Atmosphere: Nitrogen, Oxygen, and various other gases
Mars
Mars is the 4th planet from the sun and is part of the inner terrestrial planets. Mars is similar to earth in that it completes a rotation in 24.6 hours, which is 23.9 earth hours! It also has seasons, and is home to the largest volcano system in the solar system.

Classification: Planet
Star Type: Terrestrial
Moons: 2
Temperature: -153°C to 20°C
Atmosphere: Carbon dioxide, argon, and nitrogen
Jupiter
Jupiter is the 5th planet from the sun, is the first of the outer gas giants and is the largest planet in the solar system. The lines on Jupiter’s body are from the storms raging inside, including the Great Red Spot. The Great Red Spot is a storm larger than earth that has been going on for over a decade!

Classification: Planet
Planet Type: Gas Giant
Moons: 79
Temperature: -110°C
Atmosphere: Hydrogen and helium
Saturn
The planet of the rings! Saturn is 6th from the sun and is the second planet in the outer gas giants. Saturn is most well known for its 7 spinning rings made of rocks and ice. Other planets do have rings however the rings of Saturn are much larger and complex than the others!

Classification: Planet
Planet Type: Gas Giant
Moons: 83
Temperature: -140°C
Atmosphere: Hydrogen and helium
Uranus
Uranus is the 7th planet from the sun and is the third of the outer gas giants. Uranus is an ice giant meaning that it is filled with a dense fluid that is extremely icy. The planet gets its colour from the methane gas in the atmosphere. Uranus is a little odd as it rotates on its side!

Classification: Planet
Planet Type: Ice Giant
Moons: 27
Temperature: -195°C
Atmosphere: molecular hydrogen, atomic helium, and a small amount of methane.
Neptune
Neptune is the 8th and final official planet apart from our current solar system, it is also the fourth and final outer gas giant. Since Neptune is so far away, it is the only planet that cannot be seen with a naked eye. It is also filled with super sonic winds reaching 2,000 KM per hour!

Classification: Planet
Planet Type: Ice Giant
Moons: 14
Temperature: -200°C
Atmosphere: molecular hydrogen and atomic helium, and methane
Pluto
The one and only Pluto! Pluto is one of 5 dwarf planets, and is smaller than the moon we see on Earth! It is a pretty notable and popular dwarf planet as before August 24th, 2006 it was actually considered a planet. Due to the small size it got demoted however, will never be forgotten.

Classification: Dwarf Planet
Moons: 5
Temperature: -225°C
Atmosphere: Molecular nitrogen with small amounts of methane and carbon monoxide


Size and distances of the planets are not to scale. Information on this page has been retrived from NASA.Gov and may not be 100% accurate.
